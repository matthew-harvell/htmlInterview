HTML Layout Interview v2.3

These are some sample images from an image gallery. The client wishes to see these laid out in a few different ways to see what looks best to their eyes. Note that the native sizes of the images have been provided with a single size of 400px on the long side but they are not all in the same orientation. You may feel free to resize these (should you decide to do so that is) in the browser for the purpose of this exercise. Assume that the viewport will always be a laptop-sized screen (no mobile or giant 50 inch displays - 14 — 17 inch only) for the purpose of this exercise.
Below there are three div elements. These are all colored to indicate their boundaries. Within each of these elements we would like a different layout of the five images:
  1. The client would like to have all the images aligned on the top edge instead of on the bottom. Note that this might require the images to be resized.
  2. The client would like the images separated horizontally into columns with a 5px margin around each and an inset border of 2px. A shadow on the right and bottom side would be nice, use your judgement on the size and color. Perhaps some radius on the images.
  3. The client would like the images to zig-zag from upper left to the right and then back to lower left. Consider using one of the layout systems (flex, grid, etc.) General textual idea the client wrote on the back of a napkin at Shake Shack:
